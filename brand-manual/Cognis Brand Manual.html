Who we are

A global intelligence partner

Cognis Group is an AI company that builds, deploys, and governs intelligent systems that permanently transform organizations — headquartered in Lagos, engaged across Africa, Europe and the Americas.

We close the gap between AI ambition and AI execution. Where most firms stop at the slide deck, we stay until intelligent systems are in production, the workforce can run them, and governance keeps them safe.

Three practices operate under one roof — strategy, engineering, and enablement — with no handoffs and no gaps. The result is an organization that is sharper, faster, and genuinely AI-ready long after our work is complete.

02 · LogoThe Mark
The symbol

The Cognis Mark

Two geometric forms read as one gesture: an ascending peak and a forward chevron. Together they describe the brand’s entire reason to exist — upward movement and momentum, the moment something is touched and begins to change.

A

The Peak

Ascent, judgment, the human standing higher with intelligence.

B

The Chevron

Forward motion, deployment, systems moving into production.

C

The Cut

The negative gap — the gap between ambition and execution we close.

The mark may appear on its own as a standalone symbol once brand recognition is established — in app icons, avatars, favicons, embroidery and patterns. In all primary communications it appears within an approved lockup.

02 · LogoConstruction
Geometry

Built on a unit grid

The mark is constructed on a square grid of 12 units. Angles are fixed at 58° and proportions are exact — reproduce only from master artwork.

PropertyValue
Grid12 × 12 units
Stroke angle58° from horizontal
Glyph width12 units
Glyph height8 units
Inner gap1 unit minimum
Corner radius0 — hard corners only
Optical balanceCentered on visual mass, not bbox

Never redraw, trace or “rebuild” the mark. The grid is shown for understanding only.

Over photography

Place the reverse (Paper) mark over a darkened image area with at least 40% overlay. Never place the mark over busy mid-tones.

Single-colour & mono

For etching, embossing, foil or single-colour print, use 100% Ink or 100% Paper. The lime and cyan accents are never used in single-colour reproduction.
