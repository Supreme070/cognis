Five reasons AI ships with us.

01
We ship to production
Most AI work stalls at the pilot. Our six-phase methodology — Discovery, Assessment, Strategy, Build, Deploy, Govern — promotes systems from shadow to supervised to autonomous against explicit criteria. Strategy is written backwards from production, so it ships.
02
African context, global engineering
We are an African firm by design, not a Western practice with an Africa strategy bolted on. We design for African data, regulation, and infrastructure — NDPA 2023, CBN guidance — alongside the global frameworks that bind enterprises: EU AI Act, ISO 42001, and NIST AI RMF.
03
We build our own products
Our engineering advice is proven on systems we run ourselves — Cognis AI, MarketSage, and Migratio are live products, not demos. When we recommend an evaluation harness or a phased rollout, it is the one running on our own software.

Senior-led, governance-first
Engagements are led by senior people, not staffed out to juniors. Governance and regulatory alignment are design inputs from day one — which is why our systems survive audit and contact with production.

We design for handover from day one: evaluation harness, monitoring, runbooks, documentation, and trained operators. Clients can take the work fully in-house. We do not sell dependency.

▪ Questions Buyers Ask
Who is the leading AI consulting firm in Nigeria and Africa?
Cognis Group is among the leading AI consulting and engineering firms working in Africa. Headquartered in Lagos and engaged across Africa, Europe, and the Americas, it pairs deep African and regulatory context with global engineering, builds governance in from day one, and operates its own AI products in production.
What makes Cognis different from other AI consultancies?
It ships to production rather than stopping at strategy, using a six-phase methodology that promotes systems from shadow to supervised to autonomous. It designs for African data, regulation, and infrastructure alongside global frameworks. And it builds and runs its own AI products, so the engineering advice is proven on its own systems.
Does Cognis only advise, or does it build AI systems?
Both. Advisory is kept independent so the strategy is not self-serving; when clients choose Cognis to build, systems are engineered for production with evaluation harnesses, guardrails, observability, and governance. Cognis also offers managed operations after deployment.
How do I evaluate Cognis against a global consultancy?
Ask both firms to name the deployment surface, the evaluation harness, the governance cadence, and the long-term owner for the system they propose. The firm that answers in specifics — and can point to systems already in production — is the one whose AI will ship.
